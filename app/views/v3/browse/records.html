Your medical record and NHS number

How to see your medical records

There are different types of medical records.

You can see your GP records by:

  • asking for them at your GP surgery
  • going online to see them (if you’ve signed up for online appointment booking or repeat prescriptions)

Find out how to sign up for GP online services.

To see your other medical records, ask at your:

  • hospital
  • dentist
  • optician

How to see someone else’s records

You can only see someone else’s records if you’ve been given permission to do so.

Find out how to see someone else’s medical records.

How to get blood test results

Usually you'll have a blood test and the results will be sent to your GP.

You'll usually get a phone call if the GP thinks you need a follow-up appointment. Otherwise you might not hear anything.

If you do not hear anything after 2 weeks, you can always phone your GP surgery and ask them to check if your results have come in.

How to find your NHS number

You can find your NHS number on any letters you’ve had from the NHS.

Check any:

  • prescriptions
  • test results
  • hospital referral letters
  • appointment letters

If you cannot find your NHS number

Ask at your GP surgery if you cannot find your NHS number. They should be able to tell you what it is.

If you do not have a GP, you’ll need to register with a GP surgery to get an NHS number.

Find a local GP surgery to register with.

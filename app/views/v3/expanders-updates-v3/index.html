Contact the NHS

Emergency and urgent problems

If it’s an emergency:

  • call 999
  • go to A&E if you can

If you need help now, but it is not an emergency:

  • go to 111.nhs.uk
  • call 111
Speak to someone about your health

If you have a medical problem

You can:

  • speak to a pharmacist for advice on medicines or common problems like coughs, colds and rashes
  • see your GP or dentist

If you'd like to read more

You can:

  • use the health A-Z to read about conditions, symptoms and treatment information
  • use the medicines A-Z to find out how your medicine works, how and when to take it and possible side effects

Give feedback or make a complaint

How to give feedback about an NHS service

You can give feedback about any NHS care, treatment or service by:

  • speaking to a member of staff at the place you went to, such as a GP surgery or hospital
  • give a rating or review – find the place you went to to leave feedback

How to make a complaint about an NHS service

You can complain to:

  • a member of staff at the place you went to, such as a GP surgery or hospital
  • the organisation in charge of the place you went to – for example, this might be NHS England or your local clinical commissioning group (CCG)

Contact NHS England if your complaint is about:

  • a GP surgery
  • an NHS dentist
  • an NHS optician
  • a pharmacy
  • NHS 111

Contact your local CCG if your complaint is about:

  • hospital care
  • mental health services
  • out of hours services
  • community services such as district nursing

How to find your NHS number

You can find your NHS number on any letters you’ve had from the NHS.

Check any:

  • prescriptions
  • test results
  • hospital referral letters
  • appointment letters

If you cannot find your NHS number

Ask at your GP surgery if you cannot find your NHS number.

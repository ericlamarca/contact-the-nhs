Contact the NHS

Emergency and urgent problems

If it’s an emergency:

  • call 999
  • go to A&E if you can

If you need help now, but it is not an emergency:

  • go to 111.nhs.uk
  • call 111
Speak to someone about your health

If you have a medical problem

You can:

  • speak to a pharmacist for advice on medicines or common problems like coughs, colds and rashes
  • see your GP or dentist

If you'd like to read more

You can:

  • use the health A-Z to read about conditions, symptoms and treatment information
  • use the medicines A-Z to find out how your medicine works, how and when to take it and possible side effects

Give feedback about a service

How to give feedback about an NHS service

You can give feedback about any NHS care, treatment or service by:

  • speaking to a member of staff at the place you went to, such as a GP surgery or hospital
  • give a rating or review – find the place you went to to leave feedback

How to make a complaint about an NHS service

You can make a complaint about any NHS care, treatment or service by complaining to:

  • a member of staff at the place you went to, such as a GP surgery or hospital
  • the organisation in charge of the place you went to – for example, this might be NHS England or your local clinical commissioning group (CCG)

Contact NHS England if your complaint is about:

  • a GP surgery
  • an NHS dentist
  • an NHS optician
  • a pharmacy
  • NHS 111

Contact your local CCG if your complaint is about:

  • hospital care
  • mental health services
  • out of hours services
  • community services such as district nursing

If your complaint is about care you received in hospital

Speak to the Patient Advice and Liaison service (PALS) for advice.

Find your medical records and NHS number

How to see your medical records

There are different types of medical records.

You can see your GP records by:

  • asking for them at your GP surgery
  • going online to see them (if you’ve signed up for online appointment booking or repeat prescriptions)

Find out how to sign up for GP online services.

To see your other medical records, ask at your:

  • hospital
  • dentist
  • optician

How to see someone else’s records

You can only see someone else’s records if you’ve been given permission to do so.

Find out how to see someone else’s medical records.

How to get blood test results

If you have a blood test, the results are usually sent to your GP.

  • get a phone call if the GP thinks you need a follow-up appointment
  • or if everything is fine you might not hear anything

If you do not hear anything after 2 weeks, you can phone your GP surgery and ask them to check if your results have come in.

How to find your NHS number

You can find your NHS number on any letters you’ve had from the NHS.

Check any:

  • prescriptions
  • test results
  • hospital referral letters
  • appointment letters

If you cannot find your NHS number

Ask at your GP surgery if you cannot find your NHS number. They should be able to tell you what it is.

If you do not have a GP, you’ll need to register with a GP surgery to get an NHS number.

Find a local GP surgery to register with.
